Modular Manipulation: An Effective Marriage of Classical Planning, Vision, and RL

Abstract

Manipulation is a challenging problem in robotics since the robot has to reason about making and breaking contact, which is highly discontinuous and difficult to model, while controlling the arm accurately and precisely. Long horizon manipulation is even more difficult as it requires reasoning over many steps, making the problem exponentially more complex. While classical, sense-plan-act style approaches can perform long-horizon reasoning, they struggle to produce dexterous behavior. End-to-end approaches, on the other hand, struggle with long-horizon reasoning but excel at producing low-level control policies. In this work, we aim to combine the best of both worlds by taking a novel approach called Modular Manipulation (M&M): using learned modules for sensing (vision) and acting (control) while leveraging classical planning to sequence skills. We empirically demonstrate that M&M is capable of solving over 20 challenging single and multi-stage manipulation tasks from raw visual input, out-performing classical and end-to-end approaches. Our code will be made publicly available.
